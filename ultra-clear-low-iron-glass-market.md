<p><p>The Ultra-Clear Low Iron Glass market encompasses two primary types: rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is produced by rolling molten glass into sheets, resulting in a smooth finish. In contrast, float glass is formed by floating molten glass on molten tin, creating a uniform thickness and clarity. Both types exhibit reduced iron content, enhancing light transmission and color accuracy, making them ideal for applications in architecture, solar panels, and high-end displays where clarity is paramount.</p></p>

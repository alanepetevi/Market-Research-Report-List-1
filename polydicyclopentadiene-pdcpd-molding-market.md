<p><p>The Polydicyclopentadiene (PDCPD) molding market consists of various techniques, each catering to different production needs. Reactive Injection Molding (RIM) involves mixing resin and hardener to form parts. Resin Transfer Molding (RTM) injects resin into a fibrous preform. Vacuum Assisted Forming (VARI) uses vacuum pressure for mold filling, while Variable Vacuum Permeation Molding (FI) allows controlled resin flow under varying pressures. Compression Molding (SMC) shapes materials using heat and pressure, whereas Wet Molding (WCM) utilizes uncured resin. Prepreg Moulding (PCM) employs pre-impregnated fibers for enhanced properties. Other methods include specialized techniques for niche applications.</p></p>

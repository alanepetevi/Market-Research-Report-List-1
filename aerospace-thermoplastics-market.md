<p><p>The aerospace thermoplastics market is segmented into three main types. High-performance thermoplastics, such as polyphenylene sulfide (PPS) and polyether ether ketone (PEEK), offer superior mechanical and thermal properties for critical aerospace applications. Engineering thermoplastics, including polycarbonate and nylon, are used for components that require a balance of performance and cost. Standard thermoplastics, like polyvinyl chloride (PVC) and polyethylene, are utilized for non-structural applications, providing adequate functionality at a lower expense. Each category caters to specific aerospace needs and regulations.</p></p>

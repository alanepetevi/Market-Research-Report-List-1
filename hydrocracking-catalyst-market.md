<p><p>Hydrocracking catalysts are specialized materials used in the hydrocracking process, a refining technique that breaks down heavy petroleum fractions into lighter, more valuable products like diesel, jet fuel, and gasoline. These catalysts typically consist of metals like nickel, cobalt, molybdenum, or tungsten supported on materials such as alumina or silica-alumina. They play a critical role in enhancing the efficiency of hydrocracking by facilitating hydrogenation and cracking reactions under high pressure and temperature.</p><p>The Hydrocracking Catalyst Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.7% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand for cleaner fuels, stricter environmental regulations, and the need to process heavier crude oils. Additionally, advancements in catalyst formulations, such as the development of more durable and selective catalysts, are further propelling market growth.</p><p>Emerging trends in the market include a focus on bio-based hydrocracking catalysts and the integration of digital technologies for catalyst performance optimization.
